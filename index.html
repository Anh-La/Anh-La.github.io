Trusts & Small Business CGT Concession Practice Case Study 2024

Arthur Jones, a former farmer, has transferred his farming operations to his son William and established a family trust. The trust, now managing the farm and related assets, faces several tax challenges, including capital gains tax (CGT) implications from the sale of farming property, equipment, and livestock. The family is seeking advice on utilizing small business CGT concessions to minimize tax liabilities, especially in light of potential sales and influence within the trust. Additionally, professional conduct concerns regarding the use of financial information and unreported income in prior years are under review.

Small business CGT Practice Case Study 2024

Blackstone Pty Ltd, a jewelry business established in 2002, faces significant tax considerations following the sale of its business assets and City Store property. The company and its shareholders—Ted, Mary, and Barry—must navigate the capital gains tax (CGT) implications, including the availability of small business CGT concessions under Division 152. Ted's potential retirement and the contractual obligations following the sale add complexity, as does the need to manage any potential conflicts of interest for the advisor, particularly concerning Barry. Proper application of CGT discounts, management of restrictive covenants, and adherence to tax regulations are crucial for minimizing tax liabilities and ensuring compliance.

International Tax Practice Case Study 2024

SkyNet Ltd, a UK-based company, is expanding into Australia by establishing a manufacturing facility and sales offices. The company faces complex international tax issues, including determining permanent establishment (PE) status in Australia, attributing income to the Australian operations, and handling expense deductions. The potential relocation of the manufacturing facility to Singapore introduces additional tax considerations, such as PE in Singapore and implications of debt vs. equity financing. SkyNet must navigate these challenges while ensuring compliance with Australian tax laws, including transfer pricing, thin capitalization, and potential CGT on any future sale of its Australian business. Personal tax implications for key individuals relocating to Australia are also a concern, particularly in managing their residency status and worldwide income tax obligations.

GST Practice Case Study 2024

Power Australia Pty Limited is considering acquiring Solar Store Pty Limited (SStore) and is reassessing whether to purchase shares or acquire assets from its subsidiaries. The key tax concerns involve determining if the acquisition can be treated as a GST-free going concern, which would impact the GST treatment of the transaction and eligibility for claiming input tax credits (ITCs). If the acquisition does not qualify as GST-free, it will be treated as a taxable supply, affecting ITC claims. Adjustments may also be necessary for expenses already incurred based on the change in transaction structure. Ensuring proper documentation and compliance with GST regulations is essential to optimize tax outcomes and avoid complications.
